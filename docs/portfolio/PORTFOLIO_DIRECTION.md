# Anthony's Portfolio Direction

This document summarizes the current strategy for Anthony Quispilaya's professional portfolio.

## Core Promise

I am a reliable full-stack engineer who ships quickly, thinks user-first, and builds practical AI automation and systems-integration products from a strong web-development foundation.

## Target Audience

### Primary Visitor

A recruiter at a startup company hiring for a team that builds useful products for real users.

### What They Need To Believe

- I can ship working software without creating extra risk for the team.
- I am reliable, prepared, and able to communicate through delivery.
- I understand frontend, backend, databases, APIs, and real user workflows.
- I can use AI practically, not just talk about it.
- I have programming fundamentals beyond AI-assisted work.
- I care about user experience and removing friction.

## Positioning

### Main Message

Fast to ship. Careful about users. Building practical AI systems from full-stack fundamentals.

### Supporting Message

My portfolio should show that I can help a team move quickly while still being thoughtful about users, product needs, and technical responsibility.

## Vibe And Visual Direction

### Archetype Mix

- Hero: speed, delivery, momentum, proof of winning.
- Caregiver: user focus, reliability, helpfulness, reduced friction.

### Visual Style

- Clean
- Structured
- Polished
- Editorial
- Professional without feeling cold

### Avoid

- Brutalist or grungy visuals.
- Pure decoration with no proof value.
- Overly soft copy that loses momentum.
- Overly aggressive copy that feels self-centered.

1. Full-stack web development: frontend, backend, APIs, databases, product flows.
